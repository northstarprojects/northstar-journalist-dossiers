# Getting Started

The system is fully cloud-hosted — team members just need a browser. No installation required.

## Accessing the app

**URL:** https://classy-pegasus-00cc40.netlify.app

Open the link and you're in. The app is shared — everyone on the team works in the same database in real time.

---

## First-time setup checklist

### Step 1 — Add target publications

Go to **Publications** (Admin section in sidebar).

Publications are pre-seeded with 17 major outlets (TechCrunch, Wired, MIT Tech Review, etc.). Add any additional outlets relevant to North Star, or use **Suggest with AI** to have Claude recommend new ones.

Set the appropriate tier:

| Tier | Who | Examples |
|---|---|---|
| A | Major Tech & AI | TechCrunch, Wired, MIT Technology Review, The Verge |
| B | Business / Mid-tier | Forbes Tech, Fast Company, Bloomberg Technology |
| C | Regional & Niche | Hypepotamus, local outlets, AI newsletters |

### Step 2 — Discover RSS feeds

On each publication row, click the **Scan** icon (↻) to find journalists from their RSS feeds. Or expand the Feeds panel and click **Auto-discover feeds** to find all AI/tech section feeds automatically.

### Step 3 — Review journalist suggestions

Go to **RSS Suggestions** (Admin section). The RSS scanner surfaces journalists who write about AI and startups. Accept or reject each one — accepted journalists are auto-scored by Claude.

### Step 4 — Add contact info

On each journalist's profile, use **Find email via Apollo** to look up their professional email, or add it manually.

### Step 5 — Set House Style

Go to **House Style** (Admin section). Write standing instructions that Claude will follow when drafting every outreach email — your tone, what to avoid, any standard sign-off.

### Step 6 — Create your first campaign

Go to **Campaigns** → **New Campaign**. Pick a type (Cold Intro, Event, Hackathon, Founder Promo), select journalists, click **Generate Drafts**, review each draft, then mark as sent after you email them.
