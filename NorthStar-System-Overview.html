Why we built this

The Problem We're Solving

Getting covered by the right journalists is one of the highest-leverage activities for an early-stage AI startup. But the process of finding, researching, and tracking journalists is fragmented across spreadsheets, emails, and guesswork.

🔍
Discovery is manual and slow
Finding journalists who actually cover AI — not just tech broadly — requires scanning dozens of publications by hand.
📊
No structured prioritisation
Without scoring, all journalists feel equally important. We'd pitch everyone the same way, with mediocre results.
🗂️
Outreach isn't tracked
When you've already pitched someone, when to follow up, and whether they ever replied — all lived in individual inboxes.
System overview

What It Is

A journalist CRM that combines automated discovery with human review. Automation handles the tedious parts — scanning RSS feeds, scoring relevance, suggesting new sources. Humans make every final decision.

🗞️ Publication registry
A curated list of media sources, categorised by tier, with per-publication RSS feed management and automated health monitoring.
🔎 Journalist discovery
Automatically surfaces new journalists from RSS feeds and staff pages, scored for AI/startup relevance before you ever see them.
📋 Journalist dossiers
Rich records with scoring across 6 dimensions, article history, contact details, best pitch angles, and full outreach logs.
📬 Outreach tracking
Every pitch, follow-up, and response logged in one place. Follow-up reminders built in. Status tracked from first contact to coverage.

01

Publication Management

How we source, classify, and maintain our media list

Publication management

3-Tier
Classification

Every publication is classified as A, B, or C. Tier drives how we prioritise journalists and which publications we focus on first. Tier C is not lower priority — it's more targeted.

🏆Tier A → Personalised, high-effort pitch
📰Tier B → Tailored pitch with relevant angle
📍Tier C → Targeted niche/local outreach
A
Tier A — Major Tech & AI
Large-audience national publications with AI as a primary editorial focus. High domain authority, broad reach across investors, journalists, hires, and customers.
TechCrunch · Wired · MIT Technology Review · The Verge · VentureBeat · Ars Technica
B
Tier B — Business & Mid-Tier
Business publications with a dedicated tech or AI desk. Audience skews toward executives and decision-makers. Less technical, more business/strategy focus.
Forbes Technology · Fortune Tech · Fast Company · Inc. · Bloomberg Technology · WSJ Tech
C
Tier C — Regional & Niche
Regional outlets (especially Southeast US), AI-specific newsletters, and emerging verticals. Smaller but highly targeted audiences — often generates more relevant local buzz.
Hypepotamus · Atlanta Business Chronicle · AJC · GeekWire · AI Newsletters
Publication sourcing

3 Ways We Find New Publications

🤖
Claude AI — Weekly (Mon 8am)
Claude Opus 4.8 reviews our current list and suggests up to 5 new publications each Monday. It knows our tier definitions, mission, and what we already track — so suggestions are genuinely additive, not repeats.
Auto-deduplicatedHuman approval required
📦
OPML Import — Bulk
Import entire RSS feed libraries from Feedspot, Feedly, Inoreader, or any RSS reader. The system cross-checks against existing publications and routes new ones to the approval queue.
Tier auto-detectedHuman approval required
✍️
Manual Add — One-off
Add a single publication directly. RSS feed URL is optional — leave it blank and the system auto-discovers RSS via HTML link tags and 8 common feed path patterns.
Added immediatelyRSS auto-discovered
Google News virtual publications — 3 pre-seeded search feeds (AI Startups, Atlanta Tech, Generative AI) participate in journalist discovery like real publications, with no homepage to manage.
Publication management

Category Feed Discovery

Each publication can have multiple RSS feeds. A main feed + per-topic category feeds. Category feeds produce dramatically higher-quality journalist suggestions by filtering out off-topic content.

Why it matters
📰
Main feed — noisy
The Verge main feed: gadgets, gaming, entertainment, films, AND AI. Most articles are irrelevant.
🎯
Category feed — signal
The Verge /ai-artificial-intelligence feed: only AI articles. Every byline is a candidate.
Auto-discovery (⚡ button)
PublicationCategory Feed URL
The Verge/ai-artificial-intelligence/rss/index.xml
TechCrunch/category/artificial-intelligence/feed/
Ars Technica/ai/feed
Wired/feed/tag/artificial-intelligence/rss
Discovery scans the homepage for AI/startup/tech keyword links, then probes 10 RSS suffix patterns for each. Takes ~20 seconds per publication.

02

Journalist Discovery

How we find journalists automatically and filter for relevance

Journalist sourcing

The Discovery Pipeline

Method 1 — RSS Scan (Wed 7am, automatic)
1
Fetch all feeds
Main + all category feeds for every active publication
2
Extract bylines
Parse author names from dc:creator, author fields. Strip email prefixes, filter student bylines
3
Collect articles
Up to 10 articles per author from the last 90 days
4
Score relevance (0–10)
Category tags + title keywords across all articles. Best article surfaced as evidence.
5
Deduplicate & queue
Skip if already in DB, pending, or rejected in last 30 days
Method 2 — Staff Page Scan (on-demand)
1
Probe author paths
Tries /authors, /staff, /team, /contributors, /reporters, /people and more
2
Extract names
Anchor text, CSS class patterns, JSON-LD structured data
3
Add to queue
Catches journalists who haven't published recently — won't appear in RSS
⚠️ Limitation: only works on server-rendered HTML. JS-rendered pages return empty results. Firecrawl is the upgrade path.
Journalist discovery

Relevance Scoring (0 – 10)

Two scoring signals
SignalPoints
AI tag (ai, llm, openai, anthropic…)+3 per article
Startup tag (funding, VC, series A…)+3 per article
AI title keyword ("AI", "LLM", "Claude"…)+2 per article
Startup title keyword ("raises", "Series A"…)+2 per article
No categories → title weight boost×1.5
Noise tag/keyword (gaming, sports, deals…)−2 per article
Scored across up to 10 recent articles. Total clamped to 0–10. The highest-scoring article is surfaced as evidence — not the most recent.
Score bands in the review queue
🟢 High — score 6–10
Strong AI/startup coverage. Likely worth adding.
🟡 Mid — score 3–5
Mixed signal. Review their best article before deciding.
⚪ Low — score 0–2
Little to no AI/startup coverage. Bulk-skip with one click.
Example matched tags visible in the queue:
anthropicai agentsstartupsseries allm
Journalist discovery

The Review Queue

All journalist suggestions go through a human review step — nothing is added automatically. The queue is sorted by relevance score, highest first.

Each suggestion shows
👤
Name + article countHow many articles were scanned to compute the score
🏷️
Relevance score badgeGreen/amber/grey based on 0–10 score
🔖
Matched tagsSpecific AI/startup tags that contributed to the score
📄
Best article (linked)The highest-scoring article as evidence — not the most recent
Actions available
✅
Accept
Creates a draft journalist record. Claude immediately runs in background → pre-fills beat, all 6 scores, tier, and best pitch angle.
⏭️
Skip
Archives the suggestion. Won't resurface for 30 days (cooldown period).
🗑️
Skip all low-signal
One-click bulk dismiss of all suggestions scoring < 3 — clears noise fast.

✅ Approving suggestions
No publication or journalist is added automatically. Every suggestion waits in a queue for human review.
✍️ All outreach
Every pitch is human-written and human-sent. Claude helps with angle suggestions — it doesn't send anything.

Email addresses are entered manually from publicly available sources. No automated email harvesting.
